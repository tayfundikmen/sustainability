Project Sustainability Statement

Environmental & Operational Commitments for Research Practice

Tayfun Dikmen, DVM, PhD · University of Liverpool · Version 1.0  |  February 2026
This statement sets out the environmental and operational sustainability commitments that will govern the conduct of this research project. All co-investigators are considered to have read and accepted these principles prior to submission. For any personnel joining the project following award — including postdoctoral researchers, PhD students, and collaborating scientists — acceptance of this statement will form part of the onboarding process and will be considered a criterion in recruitment decisions.
Institutional Alignment

The commitments set out in this statement are designed to support and advance the sustainability objectives of the University of Liverpool. This project is conducted in alignment with the following institutional frameworks:

  • University of Liverpool Sustainability Strategy 2031 — which sets out the University's commitment to environmental and social sustainability across research, education, and operations, aligned to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.
  • UKRI Concordat for the Environmental Sustainability of Research and Innovation Practice — to which the University is a signatory, committing to reduce the environmental impacts of research and achieve the transition to sustainable practice.
  • University of Liverpool Waste Management Plan — which governs waste segregation, disposal routes, and the reduction of waste generated across the University estate.
1 Consumables & Procurement Supply Chain · Waste Reduction · Reuse

All procurement decisions will be made with environmental impact in mind. Where scientifically equivalent alternatives exist, suppliers with recognised sustainability credentials will be preferred.

  • Reusable labware will be favoured over single-use plastics where contamination risk and experimental requirements permit. Plastic consumables such as centrifuge tubes and reagent reservoirs that have not been in contact with toxic, biohazardous, or infectious materials will be washed and reused rather than discarded after a single use.
  • Reagent, antibody, and kit stocks will be managed through a shared laboratory inventory system to prevent over-ordering and waste from expiry. Items approaching their expiry date will be made available institute-wide at no cost, announced through departmental channels, so that usable materials are not discarded unnecessarily.
  • Laboratory waste will be segregated according to category — general, recyclable, chemical, and biological — and disposed of in compliance with institutional guidelines and applicable regulations.
2 Paper Use & Digital Practices Paperless Workflow · Digital Records

Paper consumption within the project will be actively minimised through the adoption of digital alternatives at every practicable stage of the research workflow.

  • An electronic laboratory notebook (ELN) will be used as the primary means of recording experimental data, protocols, and observations, replacing physical notebooks.
  • Document review, manuscript preparation, and data annotation will be conducted digitally. Printing will be reserved for circumstances where a physical copy is strictly necessary.
  • Data backup and archiving will rely on secure institutional digital storage systems rather than hard-copy records.
3 Travel Carbon-Conscious Dissemination · Transport Policy

Travel funded through this project will be subject to the following principles, applicable to all project personnel.

  • Rail travel will be used in preference to air travel wherever a viable and feasible rail connection exists between the origin and destination.
  • Air travel will be avoided where alternatives are available and feasible. Where no viable alternative exists, travel will be limited to activities of clear scientific necessity, such as presenting at international conferences or conducting essential collaborative visits.
  • Personal vehicle use will not be reimbursed from project funds for the purpose of conference attendance or collaborative travel.
4 Sustainability Training Professional Development · Shared Understanding

All project personnel are expected to complete sustainability training modules provided by the University of Liverpool through the LearnWell platform as part of their engagement with this project.

Review & Accountability

This statement will be reviewed annually and updated to reflect changes in institutional policy, funder requirements, and evolving best practice. The most current version will be made publicly available online.
